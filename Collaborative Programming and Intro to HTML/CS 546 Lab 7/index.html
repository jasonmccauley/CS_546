My Travel Destinations

New York City, New York

New York City is a paradise for baseball fans, with the iconic Yankee Stadium and its pinstripes being an integral part of the sport's history. The energy in the stadium is unmatched, especially when the Red Sox or Astros are in town and the bright lights are on. New York City can also be explored for its hidden gem restaurants, such as General Ock's Deli, which encapsulates the bodega culture only found in the heart of the city.

Outside of sports, one can take a serene stroll through Central Park, or surround themself with the bright lights of Times Square. New York City also offers the modern architecture of the Hudson Yards, with each neighborhood representing its own ethnic enclave and urban life.

  1. Yankee Stadium
  2. General Ock's Deli
  3. Central Park
  4. Times Square
  5. Hudson Yards

Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

Pittsburgh's sports culture is legendary to say the least, most notably due to Heinz Field, where the most passionate Steelers fans swing their terrible towels, creating an electric atmosphere. The city's unique geography, with its three rivers that meet at Point State Park, create a site that tourists can only marvel at.

Local restaurants such as Peppi's Sandwich Shop serve Pittsburgh's most famous sandwiches, such as the Roethlisberger, which is the perfect fuel for making a trip to the Pittsburgh Zoo, or riding up the Duquesne Incline for a scenic outlook. This city combines sports history with attractions that everyone in the family can enjoy.

  1. Heinz Field
  2. Point State Park
  3. Duquesne Incline
  4. Peppi's Sandwich Shop
  5. Pittsburgh Zoo

Myrtle Beach, South Carolina

Myrtle Beach offers a perfect blend of shopping and entertainment, while still preserving its coastal charm. Broadway at the Beach and Barefoot Landing both offer copious options for dining and shopping, while the boardwalk provides a classic beach town experience.

Murrells Inlet is serene, and rather peaceful compared to the lively tourist areas, and Ripley's Aquarium puts diverse marine life on display. Each location is not only unique, but ranges from busy tourism to tranquil seclusion.

  1. Broadway at the Beach
  2. Barefoot Landing
  3. Myrtle Beach Boardwalk
  4. Murrells Inlet
  5. Ripley's Aquarium

Boston, Massachusetts

Boston sports have a legacy like no other, with Fenway Park being one of America's most beloved ballparks, and Gillette Stadium hosting the legendary Patriots that saw decades of unrivaled success behind Tom Brady. The city breathes sports, but offers cultural experience due to its rich American history and presitigous educational institutions, such as Harvard.

Harvard Square provides insight to the academic excellence, along with the downtown street life of Boston. The Museum of Fine Arts and Boston Tea Party Ships & Museum offer a glimpse at the history Boston has to offer, much of which you have seen in a textbook. Another great balance between sports, academics, and cultural significance.

  1. Fenway Park
  2. Gillette Stadium
  3. Harvard Square
  4. Museum of Fine Arts
  5. Boston Tea Party Ships & Museum

Orlando, Florida

Whether it's Disney or Universal Studios, Orlando's theme parks are truly a magical escape from reality. Magical Kingdom is the landmark of Disney World, with its iconic blue and white castle, while the Wizarding World of Harry Potter brings the stories to life for fans, with incredible and accurate attention to detail.

Epcot is a unique park, in that it provides technological innovation through its rides, but cultural exploration through its diverse food stands. Downtown Disney is the perfect place to finish a day, offerring many shopping experiences and entertainment opportunity. Each park is truly special in the thrill and charm it has to offer.

  1. Magic Kingdom
  2. The Wizarding World of Harry Potter
  3. Hollywood Studios
  4. Epcot
  5. Downtown Disney
